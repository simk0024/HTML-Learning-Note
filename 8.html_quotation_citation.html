HTML Quotation and Citation Elemens

This chapter introduces the qoutation and citation elements, including <blockqoute>, <q>, <abbr>, <address>, <cite>, and <bdo>.

<blockqoute>

<blockqoute> element defines a section that is quoted from another source.

For example:

This is just a link to Google.com. Shall you need any help, please contact Google directly. :) Oh and I just realized the citation (web URL) is not shown in webpage. Spaces, tab and line breaks ARE NOT preserved.

<q>

<q> element defines short quotation

For example:

Just a short quotation: To be or not to be

<abbr>

<abbr> tag defines an abbreviation or an acronym. Use <abbr> to show the description for the abbreviation when mouse over

Example: APAC, CB

<address>

<address> tag defines the contact information for the author/owner of a document or an article. Contents of <address> renders in italic, and browsers will always add a line break before and after the element.

Author: SIMK0024
Email: k****@hotmail.com
Tel: +0000880088

<cite>

<cite> defines the title of a creative work (e.g. book, poem, song, movie, painting, sculpture, etc.) in italic.

Little Red Riding Hood is a European fairy tale about a young girl and a Big Bad Wolf.

<bdo>

The bdo stand for Bi-Directional Overrride, which is used to override the current text direction:

Following text will be written from right to left:

This text is written from right to left.
